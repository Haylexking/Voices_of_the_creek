About This Series

A culture woven by water, trade, and memory

The Efik people of Calabar, Cross River State, stand at the crossroads of land and sea. For centuries, they have shaped West African trade, refined a cuisine beloved across Nigeria, and preserved an oral heritage of breathtaking depth. This three-part blog series — produced for the CLA 706: Indigenous Communication Systems course at the University of Ibadan — examines their origins, cultural practices, and the urgent challenges they face in a rapidly changing world.


Each post draws on scholarly sources and cultural documentation, combining academic rigour with accessible storytelling. Images used throughout are sourced from copyright-free archives and personal documentation. Placeholder slots mark spaces where original field photographs will be inserted.
